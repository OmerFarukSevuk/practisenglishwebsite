ever, never, for, since, just, already, yet

These words are often used with the present perfect.We can use ever in questions to mean ‘at any time in your life’. We can use never(‘at no time in your life’)in negative sentences. We use for and sincewith past actions or situations which continue in the present. Forgoes with periods of time and since with moments. We use just to emphasise that something happened very recently. We use already for something that has happened earlier than we expected. We use yet with questions and negative sentences to ask if something we expect has happened, or to say that it hasn’t.

Have you ever been to Japan?
I’ve never lived in a big city.
I’ve lived here for three months/since January.
We’ve just arrived. (= We arrived only a second ago.)
I don’t need to go there. I’ve already been.
The train has already left!
Have you moved yet?
I haven’t moved yet.

The Passive

Passive Form:
Subject + be+ past participle(+ by+ agent)

We make the passive with the correct tense and form of the verb to beand the past participle of the verb.To make questions in the passive, we put the ﬁrst auxiliary verb before the subject. We use the preposition byto introduce the agent (the person or thing which does the action) We use the passive when:
• we are more interested in the action than the people who do the action.
• we do not know who exactly does the action
• it is obvious or understood who did the action.

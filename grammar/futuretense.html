be gonig to and will

We use be going tofor plans and intentions which we have already decided to do in the future. We can also use be going toto make predictions about the future, particularly when we have evidence for the prediction. We use willand won’tto make general predictions about the future. We oﬅen use think, hope,expect,etc. withwilland won’tto express our opinion. We also use will and won’twhen we decide to do something at the moment of speaking. We use will and won’t to talk about the future when it is an objective truth.

We’ve decided that we’re going to eat out.
I am going to play tennis tomorrow.
I’ve studied hard for this exam. I think I’m going to pass.
The clouds are very dark. I think it’s going to rain.
I think our next exam will be easy.
I expect she will be late. Her ﬂight was delayed.
You look tired, Dad. I’ll make the dinner tonight.
It’s my birthday next week. I’ll be seventeen.

Present Continuous and Present Simple for future

We use the present continuous to talk about future arrangements or plans that have been conﬁrmed. We use the present simple directly aﬅer time expressions like when, as soon as,until,aﬅerand before. We cannot use willwith these time expressions. We can also use the present simple to talk about the future when the action is part of a timetable or routine.

Tomorrow I’m eating out. My parents booked last week.
When I go to the shops, I’ll buy something to eat.
My class starts at 8 am tomorrow.

Future Continuous

Affirmative:
Bob will be going to the library.

Negative:
We won’t be having dinner at 6 pm tomorrow.

Question:
Will you be having breakfast at this time tomorrow?

We use the future continuous to talk about activities in progress at a particular time in the future. The activities are in progress and so they are unﬁnished.

I will be writing articles on different topics.
Robert will be reading various kinds of books.

Future Perfect

Affirmative:
She will have had lunch.

Negative:
We won’t have ﬁnished the exam by half past nine.

Question:
Will you have done your homework by 9 pm tonight?

We use the future perfectto talk about activities that will be ﬁnished by a certain time in the future.We oﬅen use the preposition by with the future perfect. It means ‘some time before’.

I will have written articles on different topics before you come.
Robert will have read various kinds of books.
